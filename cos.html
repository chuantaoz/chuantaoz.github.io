Public Sanitation Safety

Shanghai is located in the east of China, the estuary of the Yangtze River and the East China Sea. With excellent natural conditions and convenient transportation, Shanghai, which plays an important role in China, is the most economically developed city in China. It is known as the world's largest port and an important port participating in the international economic cycle.

Carbon Emission

Shanghai has implemented strict carbon emission policies to control industrial carbon emission since 2013, including quota management, total control, quota trading and verification. And Shanghai has issued the 14th five year plan for ecological and environmental protection, setting the goal of reaching the carbon peak in 2025. in order to achieve the goal, Shanghai will make efforts in four aspects: upgrading transition of industrial structure, adjusting energy structure, adjusting and optimizing the transportation structure and advocating low-carbon lifestyle.

Low Birth Rate

As the biggest city of china, Shanghai is facing a complex population problem. In 21st century, the population of Shanghai is growing rapidly because of migrants from other parts of China, and reaches more than 24 million in 2019. But the growth of the population of local people is almost 0. Shanghai has always been one of provinces with the lowest birth rate in China. The birth rate of Shanghai increased to 9.04‰ in 2016 as the two-child policy was implemented. However, the birth rate decreased again in the following years, reaching the lowest point of 6.24‰ at 2019.

Cultural Industry

In order to promote the rapid development of cultural and creative industries and enhance the competitiveness of the city's cultural industry, Shanghai issued "Several Opinions on Accelerating the Innovative Development of the City's Cultural and Creative Industries" on 14 December 2017, and the cultural and creative industries have become one of the pillar industries of Shanghai's economic development.
