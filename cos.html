Public Sanitation Safety

Shanghai is located in the east of China, the estuary of the Yangtze River and the East China Sea. With excellent natural conditions and convenient transportation, Shanghai, which plays an important role in China, is the most economically developed city in China. It is known as the world's largest port and an important port participating in the international economic cycle.

Carbon Emission

Shanghai has implemented strict carbon emission policies to control industrial carbon emission since 2013, including quota management, total control, quota trading and verification. And Shanghai has issued the 14th five year plan for ecological and environmental protection, setting the goal of reaching the carbon peak in 2025. in order to achieve the goal, Shanghai will make efforts in four aspects: upgrading transition of industrial structure, adjusting energy structure, adjusting and optimizing the transportation structure and advocating low-carbon lifestyle.

Low Birthrate

As the biggest city of china, Shanghai is facing a complex population problem. In 21st century, the population of Shanghai is growing rapidly because of migrants from other parts of China, and reaches more than 24 million in 2019. But the growth of the population of local people is almost 0. Shanghai has always been one of provinces with the lowest birth rate in China. The birth rate of Shanghai increased to 9.04‰ in 2016 as the two-child policy was implemented. However, the birth rate decreased again in the following years, reaching the lowest point of 6.24‰ at 2019.

Cultural Industry

In order to promote the rapid development of cultural and creative industries and enhance the competitiveness of the city's cultural industry, Shanghai issued "Several Opinions on Accelerating the Innovative Development of the City's Cultural and Creative Industries" on 14 December 2017, and the cultural and creative industries have become one of the pillar industries of Shanghai's economic development.

Chinese smart vacuum cleaners to sanitize world

Chinese robotic vacuum cleaner makers are ratcheting up efforts to expand their presence in overseas markets amid rising global demand. Beijing Roborock Technology Co Ltd, for instance, saw its export revenue surge 124 percent year-on-year in the first six months to 1.26 billion yuan ($194.1 million). Shanghai has spent more than $60 million on a new demonstration fever clinic

China News Network Shanghai September 30 (reporter Chen Jing) The new demonstration fever clinic project of Renji Hospital affiliated to Shanghai Jiao Tong University School of Medicine has been officially capped. The total construction area of the project is 4998 square meters. The building will officially start construction on April 4, 2021, and is expected to be put into use in December this year. The total investment of the project is more than 60 million YUAN, which is fully funded by Shanghai Construction financial resources. Upon completion, Renji Hospital will have a new "fortress" for epidemic prevention and control, improve the medical environment, and make greater contributions to protecting people's lives and health and preventing and controlling infectious diseases.

Ozone: A balancing power to ecological equilibrium

Since the COVID-19 outbreak over one and a half years ago, the global infections have topped 200 million while the death toll is nearing 4.3 million. In most places outside China, repeated lockdowns and massive vaccine rollout have failed to stop the spread of the pandemic. Under such circumstances, ozone, as an effective weapon with no side effects to inactivate the novel coronavirus, deserves more attention worldwide. On Feb. 18, 2020, during the initial stage of the epidemic, Zhou Muzhi, professor of Tokyo Keizai University and head of the Cloud River Urban Research Institute, published a paper titled "Ozone: A Powerful Weapon to Combat COVID-19 Outbreak" (hereafter referred to as the "February Zhou Paper"), calling for the use of the ozone to curb the spread of the disease. The paper has been reposted by many media outlets and platforms, thus promoting the ozone trials in combating the novel coronavirus.

Shanghai Cultural Industry Development Report 2019 released

In order to let the public understand the development of Shanghai's cultural industry in 2019, the Cultural Reform and Development Office of the Propaganda Department of the CPC Shanghai Municipal Committee, the Shanghai Cultural Business Administration and the Institute of Humanities and Arts of Shanghai Jiaotong University have jointly compiled the "2019 Shanghai Cultural Industry Development Report". This report mainly analyzes the basic data of Shanghai's cultural industry in 2018[1], summarizes the current situation and main features of the development of Shanghai's cultural industry in 2019, and makes an analysis and judgment on the future development prospects of Shanghai's cultural industry.
