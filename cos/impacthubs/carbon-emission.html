Carbon emission

Shanghai adopted the plan for peaking carbon dioxide emissions before 2025, which is five years ahead of the national plan. And this is only the first step towards our final goal, carbon neutrality.

History and current state

As the biggest city in China and also one of the biggest cities in the world, Shanghai has been developing fast. But the resulting environmental problems are more and more prominent, especially the carbon emission problem. As we can see in the follow diagrams, the carbon source in Shanghai keeps growing but the carbon sink is always the same. Industry and transportation constitute a very high proportion of carbon dioxide emission in Shanghai, which is more than 75%.

Compared with other provinces of China, The carbon footprint of Shanghai is the ninth highest, but the per capita carbon footprint is the highest, since Shanghai has more highly developed economies and high per capita GDP. And Shanghai also has strong relationship with some inland provinces of abundant resources like Hebei, Shanxi, and Inner Mongolia and transfer large amounts of embodied CO2 to these provinces. So Shanghai must undertake more emission responsibility.

And compared with other big cities in the world, the carbon footprint of Shanghai is the sixth highest, but the per capita carbon footprint is lower than other big cities with developed economics and large population like Seoul, New York and Los Angeles but higher than Tokyo. There is still much to do.

Policies

Shanghai has implemented strict carbon emission policies to control industrial carbon emission since 2013, including quota management, total control, quota trading and verification. And Shanghai has issued the 14th five year plan for ecological and environmental protection, setting the goal of reaching the carbon peak in 2025. in order to achieve the goal, Shanghai will make efforts in four aspects: upgrading transition of industrial structure, adjusting energy structure, adjusting and optimizing the transportation structure and advocating low-carbon lifestyle.

Driven by these policies, Shanghai is developing cleaner production, clean energy and public transit priority.

Personal carbon allowances?

The Industry carbon emission can be controlled through quota management, how about personal carbon emission? Although it's not a large part of total emission, it still accounts for more than 5 percentage and keep growing. Personal carbon allowances or end-user emissions trading is setting a limit on Individuals carbon emission and individuals will consume their emissions credits when they make carbon-related purchases, such as electricity, fuel or public transport. And Individuals who exceed their allocation would be able to purchase additional credits from those who use less, so individuals that are under allocation would profit from their small carbon footprint.

It is a ideal policy that hard to implement. But with strong and efficient government and united people, Shanghai may have a chance to turn this dream to reality.
