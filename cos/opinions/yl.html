Achieve effective linkage between public health services and medical services

For a long time, China has implemented the policy of payment before treatment, which is conducive to guiding patients to rational drug use and safeguarding the rights and interests of both doctors and patients. However, when a variety of unknown diseases and the need to advance huge medical costs, will lead to some patients give up treatment. Therefore, it is recommended to ensure that medical institutions treat patients first and charge them later in emergencies such as outbreaks of epidemic diseases.

To ensure better implementation of the policy of first treating major diseases, we also need to improve the settlement system for medical insurance in other places and explore exemptions from medical fees for certain diseases. At the same time, we will coordinate the use of medical security funds and public health services, increase the proportion of payments to community-level medical institutions, and achieve effective linkage between public health services and medical services. In addition, supervision should be strengthened, and tracking and auditing of special funds for epidemic prevention should be conducted. Local governments should disclose to the public, in accordance with laws and regulations, information on the use of special funds, the structure of medical expenses, and awarding and subsidizing institutions.
