Social Assimilation and Decline in Birth Rate of Temporary Rural-To-Urban Migrants:A Case Study of Shanghai

Based on the Shanghai Floating Population Dynamics Monitoring Database in 2012, the discrete-time Logo model is used to analyze the impact of the social integration of the rural and urban floating population on second-child births. The study found that: stable housing in urban areas, pension insurance for urban employees, reading and newspaper learning activities, no more rural contracted land in the hometown, and longer living time in the inflow area will reduce all or part of the rural-urban migrant population The probability of having a second child. This is because the rural migrant women’s life experiences and experiences in urban areas have changed their traditional concept of childbearing, and the social paradigm of urban residents has also changed the belief of rural-urban migrants in giving birth to children; The upward social mobility of the migrant population will drive them to invest more energy and capital to improve their social class status, thus reducing the number of children born.

Language: Chinese

Authors: LIANG Tong-gui.

Publication Date: 2018

Subject Terms: temporary rural-to-urban migrants/social assimilation/discrete-time Logit Model
