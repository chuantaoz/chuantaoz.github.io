Han Han

Han Han was born on 23 September 1982 in Tinglin Town, Jinshan District, Shanghai. In 1999, he won the first prize in the first National New Concept Essay Competition with his essay "Peeping in the Cup". 2000, when he was in his first year of high school, Han Han dropped out of school and published his first full-length novel "Triple Door"; in 2001, he published the anthology "One Degree Below Zero", which won the first place in the national best-seller list that year; in 2002, he published the novel "Like a Teenager Flying"; in 2003, he started his professional racing career; in March 2004, he published the anthology "Han Han Five Years". In 2005, he launched a blog and started blogging; in September 2006, he released his debut album "Cold - 18 Forbidden"; in 2009, he edited "The Soloist"; in 2010, Han Han appeared on the cover of Time magazine; in 2011, he published a collection of essays "Youth"; in June 2012, Han released the app "ONE". In June 2012, Han Han released the app "One-one"; in 2013, he published "One: Nice to See You"; in July 2014, he directed the film "Aftermath", which was released in mainland China. 2015, he was the executive producer of the youth film "Hourglass". 28 January 2017, Han Han wrote and directed his second film On 28 January 2017, Han Han wrote and directed his second film "Ride the Wave", which was released in theatres. In 2019, Han Han's third film, "Life of Speed", was released.
